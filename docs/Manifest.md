# FishWithLocals Manifest

## Why We Exist

FishWithLocals exists to connect travellers with real local captains who know the sea. It is about people, not simply trips, dates or boats.

Choosing a captain from far away is difficult. A photograph of a boat says very little about the person at the helm. It does not tell you whether they are patient with a child holding a rod for the first time, whether they enjoy teaching, whether they will speak honestly when the weather turns, or whether they treat the sea with care. Those things should be visible before the first conversation begins.

Trust should not have to be improvised between strangers. Every captain presented by FishWithLocals is personally known and carefully verified. Their profile should show truthfully who they are and what kind of day they offer. Traveller and captain can then speak directly and decide what feels right.

Fishing is part of the experience, but it is not the whole of it. A day at sea may also hold a quiet departure before the town wakes, a lesson about currents, a story attached to a headland, a simple meal, an old method, a sudden change in weather or a long stretch without a bite. The sea, local knowledge, food, stories and people matter just as much as what is caught.

## What We Believe

We begin with people because a boat cannot welcome a guest, teach a child or judge when it is wiser to turn home. Boats matter, and they must be safe and suitable, but they remain part of a captain’s working life rather than the centre of the story. The person comes first.

Character is more useful than polished marketing. We would rather describe a captain accurately than make everybody sound impressive in the same way. Humour, patience, directness, curiosity and generosity cannot be reduced to sales language. Small details often tell the truth best: the coffee made before departure, the music played on board, what happens when the fish are not biting, and the meal a captain likes to prepare from a modest catch.

Trust must come before any transaction. FishWithLocals does not ask a traveller to choose on the strength of anonymous claims. We meet captains, listen carefully, check what should be checked and remain responsible for what we publish. The platform opens a door to conversation; it does not replace the judgement and understanding that grow through speaking directly.

Stories reveal more than listings. A list can state a duration, capacity and price, and those details should always be clear. But facts alone cannot explain a person’s relationship with a place. We want each profile and destination to carry enough lived experience that a visitor understands something of the day before deciding whether it is for them.

Respect for guests, captains, people working at sea, local customs and the limits of the water matters more than extracting the greatest profit from a trip. Transparent prices and honest advice are part of that respect.

Quality must remain ahead of growth. We would rather represent a few captains properly than build a catalogue of strangers. Expansion is worthwhile only while personal verification, careful writing and real relationships continue.

The sea deserves gratitude because it gives work, food, memory and perspective while owing us nothing. Gratitude is expressed through conduct, not grand claims. It appears in restraint, attention and the willingness to leave the water in better condition than we found it.

## What We Will Never Become

FishWithLocals will not become another booking marketplace built around filling calendars. It will not become a price-comparison site that encourages travellers to treat captains as interchangeable. Price should be transparent, but the cheapest option should never become the organising principle.

We will not create a race for reviews or turn hospitality into a contest for stars. We will not rank captains against one another, reward whoever attracts the most clicks or suggest that popularity is the same as quality. Each captain should be understood on their own terms.

We will not allow algorithms to decide who deserves attention. We will not become an advertising machine, sell priority placement or shape profiles around what keeps people scrolling. Technology may help the platform remain useful and dependable, but it must stay in service of human judgement.

Growth is welcome only while every captain receives proper care. If it weakens our standards or flattens their individuality, it has come at the wrong price.

## The Captain Comes First

A captain is responsible for the mood, judgement and rhythm of the day. Their knowledge reaches beyond finding fish to winds, changing water, safe approaches to shore and courtesies shared by people working nearby.

Personality belongs in the profile because it belongs on the boat. Some teach every knot patiently; others bring dry humour, cook, tell stories or let the sea provide the silence. Values, humour, food, patience, teaching and local knowledge all shape the experience.

We want visitors to choose a human being whose company and approach feel right. The boat is important information, especially where safety and comfort are concerned, but it is not the reason a day becomes memorable.

## Respect for the Sea

Responsible fishing is not a badge awarded once and displayed forever. It is visible in ordinary decisions made repeatedly.

It can mean releasing a fish when that is the right thing to do, respecting seasons and never taking more than is needed. It can mean carrying a rubbish bag on board, collecting floating plastic rather than passing it, or bringing an abandoned net back to shore when it can be recovered safely. It also means respecting other boats, giving working crews room and taking care around fishing lines already in the water.

Together, these actions show a captain’s relationship with the sea better than a promise. We will make them visible without turning them into a performance, and remain willing to learn.

## Success

Inquiries matter because they show that a traveller wants to begin a conversation. They are not, by themselves, the measure of whether FishWithLocals is doing good work.

Success is when travellers return home with stories they still tell years later. It is when children leave loving the sea more than when they arrived. It is when captains read their profiles and feel accurately represented rather than packaged for sale. It is when local communities are respected, visitors understand where they are, and people want to return because a genuine connection was made.

Success also means saying no when something does not fit, correcting mistakes plainly and growing only while trust remains personal.

## Final Thought

The sea does not need us to make it more impressive. Our task is simpler: to introduce people well, tell the truth about those who know the water, and leave enough space for a real day to unfold between them. If we can do that with care, FishWithLocals will have a reason to endure.
